Easy Block Patterns

First up, I'll be going through making a "Shoo-Fly Block". Real simple one, involving 5 solid tiles and 4 HST's (Half-Square Triangles)

To start, we'll need to decide a seam size and the overall size of our block.
A good standard is a 3 inch block, with a quarter inch seam. Many quilting guides use these measurements as it's big enough that the assembled block makes a nice quilt, and small enough that you can put a good few together and make a decent design.

Further, the Shoo-Fly is a great block to start with as you get practice with cutting, assembling HST's, and finally the sewing portion.

The first tiles to cut will be the full squares preferably-- the easiest by far too. For this tutorial they'll be 3.5 by 3.5 inches This is just the finished size, 3, plus double the seam for both edges.

Cut yourself 4 tiles of a background color, and 1 tile of a primary/foreground color. Quilting really is just alot of geometry regarding these sizings, so it helps to plan these out in advance. Those 4 tiles could be each measured one by one, or a row measuing 14 inches by 3.5 which you cut 3 more times to make 4 tiles. Or one big 7 by 7 tile you cut twice for 4 tiles total. Figure out what works best for you, and go from there.

I swear, the moment I figured this out I was astonished! Worked like magic, and makes total sense geometrically.
You can make 8 HST's via this method:

  1. Take two pieces of fabric, measuring 9x9 inches each
  2. Lay them on eachother, such that the topsides of each are touching on the inside. This is the side the design is on, and typically where the color is richest.
  3. Draw your diagonal lines, each corner to the opposite
  4. Sew your seams a quarter inch from those diagonals
  5. Finally, cut horizontally and vertically.
Unfurl those and be pleasently surprised! There's alternative ways to make 2 at a time, or even 16.
The 8x8 method is quite simple, so that's why I illustrated that rather than the 4.
To make 4 at a time instead, only do half such that you'd only make a single horizontal cut.

There's a handy site here which has charts and a few more methods to peruse.

Finally, to assemble your pieces!

  1. Take your first row or column of pieces, however your brain works best
  2. Lay the second piece over the first, the "tops" together, aka the colored faces inside
  3. Sew the proper side, a quarter inch away from the edge. That's what the seam is
Unfurl those and be pleasently surprised! Again! Your first two connected pieces. Repeat this proccess to connect the third piece to the end of the second.
Finally, when you are ready to connect all of your rows, you'll do the same approach. Tops together, and it helps to plan in advance using sewing pins to create spines.

The final, total Shoo-Fly block should be 9.5 by 9.5 inches -- the quarter seams plus the size of the finished tiles, 3 inches.

The first step to making a quilt is making tons of blocks, and this is exactly it. Every block connected the same way we joined these prior tiles.
